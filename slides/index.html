7 Ways

To Make Sure No One Ever Sees Your Story Online

with Lisa Williams
@lisawilliams
Audience Engagement Editor, WGBH News

1. File late.

Like, really late.If you want to score bonus points, file after 4PM.

2. File on the same thing that everyone else is filing on.

But, and this is important: DO NOT BE THE FIRST REPORTER TO FILE A STORY ON THE TOPIC. Give other news organizations at least 2 hours lead time online.

3. Mitigate Twitter Risk

If you want to make sure your story doesn't get seen, but you still want to use Twitter to talk to other journalists, here are a few tips.

Avoid consistency.

Tweet twelve times in an hour. Then go radio silent for a week.

Don't use links.

If you want to tweet about your story but be sure no one sees it, it's easy: just don't link to it.

Link to other news organizations' sites, but not your own

For bonus points, tweet links to news stories from other news organizations. If people ask you if you work there, you're doing it right.

Live tweet...but don't follow up

Livetweet from a breaking news story if you must. But never follow up with a link, or give people the impression that there might be a link coming.

4. Challenge yourself to do a story with no news hook at all.

5. Don't take photos.

But if you have to, here are a few tips.

People love faces. Make sure no one can see one.

If you must take a picture, be sure your subject is centered in the photo, far away, backlit, and has a facial expression not appropriate to the story's theme.

Write a great story. Then illustrate it with a picture of a building.

A stock photo of city hall is okay, but only if you can't get a bad picture of City Hall at night taken with your phone.

Use CMS camouflage.

Pick a photo from the CMS that's being used for another story on the same day on the same topic. The reader will be too confused to know there's a new story!

Choose a picture that makes people recoil.

Make sure they know not to go anywhere near your story with a picture that makes them unfollow you completely.

6. Write a bad headline.

Once you're sure you've got a bad headline, add six words to it.

7. Don't talk to the digital team.

The digital team will probably do things like get better lead art for you, social, graphics, etc. Huge risk!
